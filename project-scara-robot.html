Design of a Soft Robotic Gripper

As the kinematics and simulation lead on a team tasked with designing a 4-DOF SCARA robot under real-world constraints, I took ownership of the robot’s motion analysis entirely in MATLAB. I derived the forward kinematics equations to map joint variables (q₁–q₄) to end-effector position and solved the closed-form inverse kinematics for arbitrary targets. I then computed the effective workspace volume and evaluated kinematic quality metrics, such as manipulability and condition number, across that workspace. For dynamics, I formulated the equations of motion using the Euler-Lagrange approach and validated joint torque requirements under varying payloads. Finally, I implemented and tested two path-planning schemes, linear/parabolic blending and cubic-spline trajectories,  by scripting trajectory generation, timing, and sampling routines. Every simulation and analysis, from kinematics through dynamics and path planning, was scripted in MATLAB (no Simulink) to ensure full control and reproducibility
